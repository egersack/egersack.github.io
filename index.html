Average Speed vs. Distance

Pictured above is my average speed graphed against the distance I run. Each point is an individual workout. As expected, my speed is decreasing the farther I run.

What can I do with this data? I can use this graph to improve my worout habits. In the future, I hope to decrease the magnitude of the slope of this line. Hopefully I can maintain a faster pace even through my longer runs. I also hope to be hitting higher speeds while still maintaining a consistent pace.

I hope to do more longer distance runs to make my data more credible. I need more points in the higher distance range to improve my line of best fit and make my data slightly more accurate. I have also considered taking out the outlier runs which include recovery runs or maybe slow paced runs that I did with friends. Although, this would not be a real representation of my running habits.

Max Speed vs. Distance

Here is my max speed plotted against the distance I ran. The line of best fit is increasing as the distance increases.

I think that this data is very interesting. I do not think this gives me much insight into my performace because I am not interested in my max speed. However, it is nice to see that i am able to hit those higher speeds on my longer runs. I hope this means I have a good sprint.

In the future, It would be cool to do more print workoiuts and incorporate those into my code. I ould like to see how that woudl affect my line of best fit.

Average Speed vs. Date

This graph shows my average speed plotted against the date. The frist run is back in October 29, 2020. The line of best fit has a negative slope meaning my runs are getting slower over time.

Sadly, I am getting slower. I don't think I have been running as much since I got to MIT. I also think I am slightly faster during fall season because our training is more focused on endurance. I hope to stop the downward trend of this graph.

This data is very important for my performance. It is probably the most significant information I could have.
